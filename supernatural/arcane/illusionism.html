Illusionism

Illusionism, of all the ‘schools’ of magic, is considered that most closely associated with the mind: its power laying at the interplay of arcane power and a being’s perception and its anchoring into reality. Its practice, too, is dependent on the mind of the caster - being described as requiring a ‘spark’ or natural aptitude that cannot be overcome through study and practice. This has long led magical scholars to believe that illusionism lies at the crossroads of arcane magic and the mysterious and semi-mythical power of psionicism - the so-called ‘magic of the mind’. This belief, along with the peculiarities of mastering this school have led to a distinct class of magic user - the ‘illusionist’ - to emerge: those with the natural aptitude - the ‘spark’ - needed to truly explore the power of illusionism.
