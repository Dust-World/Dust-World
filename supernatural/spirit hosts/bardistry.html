Bardistry

The divide between the spirit hosts of sapient species and that of nature is not as clearly cut as clerics and druids would have people believe: sapient creatures are not elevated by merits of their culture, passions and beliefs - they are still trussed and incorporated into the natural world, following the cycles and patterns of life that are particular from place to place. Bards recognize this dichotomy - they stand with one foot in each world and their magic is created by the middle ground: by both the intangible patterns and structures that bind sapient creatures together such as language and culture, plus the life that infests even the most pristine edifices of ‘civilization’: from the filthiest rat mite to the most noble charger.
