Druidism

Though the lives of sapients are rife with spiritual energy: incandescent in their trajectory through a comparatively small period of history - the natural world has a potency all of its own. Where the spirit hosts of long-lived faiths and ancient cults may pulse bright against the entropic night of the void, there is a low, humming background illumination developed over a scale of time that cannot be comprehended by most sapient creatures - measured in thousands of years - contributed to by every living thing interconnected into a larger web of life. From the mightiest predatory fauna to the tiniest monocellular flora, each tithes over a (to clerics of sapient orders) mote of energy to the spiritual impression of that place. Even sapient creatures - when they live and die in a place - will be linked to that place. Druids are the shepherds of such sprawling, lambent hosts - carrying the inescapable interconnectivity of nature in their magic and championing the places they are spiritually connected to in their deeds.
