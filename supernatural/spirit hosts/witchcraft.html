Witchcraft

Divine magic users are described as ‘channeling’ the power of the spiritual hosts to which they are attuned. This attunement is either spontaneous - as with the ‘bespirited’ - those whose passions and beliefs are pushed through such a forceful personality as to be made manifest - or developed through aligning one’s own way of life with that the host the cleric or druid feels a connection with. Witches, spirit-eaters, warlocks - they are given many epithets - wrest this power in another way - by force. Witches utilize a system of sacrifice and minor arcane power to siphon (at best) or consume (at worse) spiritual energy from whichever host they have chosen to victimize: in the East, these hosts are often those of the community that harbours them. There are many tales of such ‘spiritual parasites’ being cast out or ‘purified’ through ordeal or execution and many traditions of folklore contain witches whose machinations brought gradual wasting to peoples and places as they devoured the sacrifice and effort of their peers for their own selfish ends.

But beyond these popular narratives, more esoteric knowledge and tales from the fringes, or places plagued with malign spiritual phenomena such as the Plague Delta, hold witches in regard as ‘sin eaters’ - those who seek out and predate solely on malign spiritual energy, weakening the power of atrocity and coaxing the restless dead to peace through the dissipation of the animus that holds them to life.
